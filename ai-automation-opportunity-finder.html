Prioritization Method

What makes a strong AI automation candidate?

The best first pilots combine meaningful value with repeatable work, accessible information, measurable outcomes, and manageable risk.

📈

Impact

Frequency, duration, number of people, and alignment with the primary business goal.

🧩

Feasibility

Standardization, exception rate, digital access, information format, and workflow complexity.

🚦

Readiness

An owner, representative examples, a baseline, a human reviewer, and an approved tool.

🛡️

Risk

Data sensitivity, decision impact, security, error consequences, and required oversight.

How the priority score works

The score is directional, not a technical or financial guarantee. It favors opportunities that are valuable, feasible, ready, and comparatively lower risk. Always validate the result with representative examples and a controlled pilot.
