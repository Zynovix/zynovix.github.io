FAQ

AI policy generator questions

An AI acceptable use policy explains how employees and contractors may use artificial intelligence at work. It defines approved tools, allowed and prohibited uses, data rules, human review, disclosure, security, and incident reporting.
The generator creates an educational starting draft, not legal advice. Whether a policy becomes binding depends on local law, contracts, communication, acknowledgement, and organizational procedures. Obtain qualified professional review.
No company or policy information is submitted to a server. The draft is generated locally in your browser. Only your color-theme preference may be stored on the device.
A practical policy should cover scope, approved tools, permitted use cases, prohibited activities, confidential and personal data, human oversight, accuracy checks, disclosure, intellectual property, vendor security, incidents, training, and regular review.
The right frequency depends on risk and regulatory context. Many organizations choose a six- or twelve-month cycle and also review the policy after material incidents, tool changes, new use cases, or legal developments.
