Prompting Basics

What the challenge teaches

Strong prompts are not about magic words. They make the task, evidence, output, and limits easier to understand.

01

Clear direction

Define the goal, audience, context, desired format, and success criteria instead of relying on vague adjectives.

02

Trustworthy grounding

Identify the source of truth, label unknowns, protect sensitive data, and verify facts that can change.

03

Focused iteration

Evaluate against a rubric and change the weakest part while preserving what already works.
