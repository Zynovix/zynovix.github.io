What the score means

The score organizes a shortlist. It does not replace hands-on testing, procurement review, security due diligence, accessibility review, or contract analysis.

Use case · 36%

How directly the product supports the primary job selected.

Budget · 18%

Whether a product's broad entry model matches budget comfort.

Team · 14%

Fit for solo use, small teams, larger teams, or enterprise rollout.

Context · 20%

Existing ecosystem alignment plus the requested data-control approach.

Requirements · 12%

The share of selected must-have capabilities represented in the profile.

Important interpretation rules

  • Fit is contextual: a lower-ranked product can be better after a real data, integration, legal, or usability review.
  • Features are plan-dependent: API, administration, governance, support, and data options may require different subscriptions or contracts.
  • Pricing is deliberately broad: the builder does not estimate taxes, usage, implementation, training, integration, support, or switching costs.
  • No paid ranking: the calculation uses only the visible profile and the fixed matching method.
Each result links to an official vendor product or pricing page. Recheck that source, security documentation, privacy terms, data-processing terms, accessibility information, and current contract before making a decision.
